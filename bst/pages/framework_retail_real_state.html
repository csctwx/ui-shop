What makes the best locations?
It's all about...
  • Demographics
  • Strength of center within trade area
  • Location within the corridor, central business district ("CBD") and shopping center
  • Relative strength of anchor stores
  • Strength of specialty tenants
  • Visibility
  • Signing opportunities
Site selection criteria
  • 50% of population is below 35 years
  • Average household income is less than $40,000
  • Greater than market average is minority based
  • Hispanic population is greater than 20% of total population in market area
Retail Corridor
  • Middle & lower tier
  • Center type: CBD, neighborhood centers, community centers
Site Location
  • Preferred co-tenancy: grocery stores, dollar stores, check cashers, beauty supply stores
  • Wireless competitors (T-Mobile, Cricket, Metro PCS, Verizon) in same center/trade area is OK
  • High-visibility vehicle or foot traffic area
  • Central business district is at or near traffic light
  • Size: 800-1,200 sq. ft.
  • Prefer end cap locations
  • Full ingress/egress
  • Adequate parking field for location
  • Sign band and criteria to allow for 24" minimum Boost sign
Lease Terms
  • Initial term 1-3 years with options
  • Occupancy rates considered by metro market area
  • TI $ dependent on new location or condition of current space
