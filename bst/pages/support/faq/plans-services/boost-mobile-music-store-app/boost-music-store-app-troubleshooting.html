How do I get Boost Mobile Music Store onto my Android handset?

You can go to Google Play Store to download the new music store. Make sure any previous versions of the app are uninstalled and you are using a Boost device.

(top)

How do I know if I have the latest version of the Boost Mobile Music Store Android App installed?

Visit the app page on Google Play. If the top button says "Open", you have the latest version. If it says "Update", there is a new version available. Click "Update" to install the latest version.

(top)

The Boost Mobile Music Store Android App stopped responding/froze.

Tap the Android "Home" key, open the app tray and open "Settings". Go to "Applications / Running". Tap on the Boost Mobile Music Store app, and hit "Stop" or "Force Stop". Tap OK when prompted for confirmation. Go back to the app tray and re-launch the Boost Mobile Music Store app.

(top)

The Boost Mobile Music Store app still won't start even after I force stopped the app.

Go to Android "Settings / Applications / Running". Tap on the Boost app, and hit "Stop" or "Force Stop". Tap OK when prompted for confirmation. Tap on "Clear Cache", and OK on the confirmation prompt. Go back to the app tray and re-launch the Boost Mobile Music Store app.

(top)

The Boost Mobile Music Store app still won't start even after I cleared the cache.

Go to Android "Settings Applications". Tap on the Boost Mobile Music Store app, and tap "Uninstall". Tap OK when prompted for confirmation. Tap OK once more when uninstall is complete. Re-download and reinstall the Boost Mobile Music Store app from Google Play.

(top)

I can't find my music.

Downloaded content will be stored in /storage/sdcard0/Music/lwmmedia. You may access and play music purchased and downloaded through the Boost Mobile Music Store app via the built-in Android "Music" player app. If you have a file manager app installed, you may also browse through the file system directly to that path to find your music. To import your music into the Boost Mobile Music Store app, select "Settings" from the main menu and then "Synchronize your music library".

(top)

I am having connection issues/slow connection/spotty connection.

If there is a Wi-Fi hotspot nearby, turning on Wi-Fi will generally solve most connection issues and speed up the connection and downloads. You may need to restart paused or in-progress downloads.

(top)

How do I find info requested by Customer Care for trouble shooting?

Go to Android "Settings", scroll to the bottom and tap on "About Phone". You will find necessary troubleshooting info such as Device model, Android version, phone number (a.k.a. MDN), MEID, etc. Please provide this info to Customer Care.
