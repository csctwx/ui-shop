Can I still activate on the Family Plan?

The Boost Mobile Family Plan will continue to be available for activation only until February 18th, 2015.

(top)

If I am still on a Family Plan, can I add secondary subscribers to the plan?

You can continue to add secondary subscribers to the primary’s account only if you are in an eligible market (Chicago, Dallas Fort-Worth, Los Angeles, Atlanta, Hawaii, Baltimore, Houston, or Washington DC).

(top)

What does the Family Plan offer include?

Each Family Plan subscriber gets unlimited talk, text and data for just $100 per month, all on the Nationwide Sprint Network. High-speed 3G/4G data is limited to the first 1GB of use.

(top)

Is International Connect available with the Family Plan?

Yes, you can still take advantage of the Family Plan and add International Connect. Each individual line will be responsible for paying for International Connect or any other add-on that is added to his/her line.

(top)

Who is responsible for paying the $100 monthly payment amount?

The primary subscriber is responsible for paying the full $100 plus his/her own individual add-ons. The secondary subscribers are only responsible for paying for any add-ons they add to their respective accounts.

(top)

Is the monthly payment date the same for the primary and secondary subscriber?

The primary subscriber's payment date is the same day of the month as when the first monthly payment was made. The payment date for each secondary subscriber is the day of the month each activated. Primary and secondary subscribers can, but do not need to, have the same payment date.

(top)

Can the secondary subscriber have the same payment date as the primary subscriber?

Yes, as long as the secondary subscriber activates on the same day as the primary subscriber. Each secondary subscriber will still be responsible for any add-ons on his/her account. If the secondary subscriber has add-ons, he/she will need to have funds available on his/her monthly payment date in order for the secondary line service to remain active.

(top)

How much does the Family Plan cost if I have fewer than 4 lines?

The Family Plan is priced at $100 per month regardless of how many lines are on it. It's recommended to activate all four lines. Keep in mind, the $100 Family Plan monthly cost does not include the price of any add-ons a subscriber may add. Nor does it include any incidentals or other third-party costs incurred.

(top)

Do I have to activate all 4 lines at once?

You do not need to activate all four lines at once, however the primary subscriber accounts must be created before 2/18/2015. Secondary subscribers can be added to the primary’s account after 2/18/2015 only if you are in an eligible market (Chicago, Dallas Fort-Worth, Los Angeles, Atlanta, Hawaii, Baltimore, Houston, or Washington DC).

(top)

How long is Family Plan available?

Beginning 2/18/2015, Boost will no longer offer the Family Plan to new primary line customers. Secondary line subscribers can continue to be added to existing primary accounts. All customers can keep their Family Plan for as long as they keep their Boost account active and remain on the Family Plan.

(top)

Will I be able to keep my Family Plan after it is no longer available for new customers?

Although we reserve the right to change, extend or end plans and offers at any time, as long as you keep your accounts active and don't change to another plan, Family Plan will continue to be available to you.

(top)

What is required to add a secondary line?

Adding a secondary line requires the phone number and security PIN of the primary line. It's recommended that the primary line account holder be present when a secondary line is being added to a Family Plan.

(top)

I went to a Boost Mobile store and they activated only my primary line. How do I activate my secondary lines?

Call Boost Customer Care (1-888-BOOST-4U1-888-BOOST-4U FREE) to activate all secondary lines.

(top)

What happens to the secondary lines if the primary subscriber leaves Boost?

Boost Customer Care will contact the secondary subscribers in approximately one business day to determine whether a new primary line needs to be set or if all secondary lines should move to regular individual Boost plans.

(top)

What happens to the primary line if one or more of the secondary lines leaves Boost?

The primary line will continue to be responsible for the $100 and can add new secondary lines to replace the previous secondary lines.

(top)

What if I want to change my phone?

Subscribers can change their phones by calling Customer Care.

(top)

Is Family Plan available nationwide?

Beginning 2/18/2015, Boost will no longer offer the Family Plan to new primary line customers. Customers in the following markets can activate new secondary line subscribers: Chicago, Dallas Fort-Worth, Los Angeles, Atlanta, Hawaii, Baltimore, Houston, or Washington DC.
Secondary line subscribers can be added to existing primary accounts. All customers can keep their Family Plan for as long as they keep their Boost account active and remain on the Family Plan.

(top)

Can the primary subscriber or any of the secondary subscribers live outside an eligible market?

Yes, they can. However, they can only activate at a participating dealer in an eligible market with an eligible area code and prefix.

(top)

Can my Family Plan be serviced outside of an eligible market?

Boost Customer Care will be able to assist Family Plan subscribers regardless of where they are.

(top)

If I lose or damage my phone and buy another to replace it, will I lose my Family Plan?

No, you will not lose your Family Plan.

(top)

Is the family plan restricted to certain phones?

All current Boost phones are eligible for the Family Plan except the BlackBerry 8330, which is no longer actively being sold.

(top)

Is my Family Plan restricted to family members only?

No, Family Plan is not restricted to family members only.

(top)

Will secondary subscribers have access to their accounts through My Account?

Yes, each subscriber will have access to his/her respective account via My Account.

(top)

Will Family Plan subscribers be able to track how much data is used in My Account?

Yes, each subscriber can track his/her own data usage.

(top)

I want to port my number, but the area code and prefix is not showing up as eligible for the Family Plan. What can I do?

You will need to choose a new number from an eligible area code and prefix to activate the Family Plan. Note: after 2/18/2015, you will only be able to add new secondary lines to existing primary subscriber accounts. If you're in an eligible market (see above), see your local Boost independent wireless retailer for details.

(top)

Are Family Plan subscribers eligible for the referral credit?

No, the Family Plan is not eligible for a referral credit. Neither the primary, nor the secondary subscribers can get a referral credit at this time.
