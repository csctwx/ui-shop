How do I sign up for the $5 Todo Mexico Plus with Mexico Roaming add-on?

The $5 Todo Mexico Plus with Mexico Roaming add-on is available only for any eligible Boost Monthly plan. If you are a Boost monthly plan customer, you can sign up for the add-on or upgrade your plan via My Account or through your local authorized dealer.

REMEMBER, adding the add-on will increase the TOTAL cost of your monthly payment amount (monthly plan cost + add-on cost). Also, this is a recurring plan add-on; it will remain attached to your monthly plan and account until you remove it.

How and when do I need to pay for the add-on?

The $5 Todo Mexico Plus with Mexico Roaming add-on is a recurring monthly plan add-on, with the add-on cost being added to the total amount due for your monthly plan services. The new total plan cost must be paid at the same time you pay for your monthly plan services and is due on your existing monthly payment due date. If your account balance cannot cover both the cost of your monthly plan services AND the $5 Todo Mexico Plus add-on (or any other recurring plan add-ons, if any), by your monthly payment due date, all of your services will be interrupted until sufficient funds to cover the total costs of your plan cost and additional services, if any, are added to your Account.

What do I have to do to enable my compatible device to roam in Mexico?

When you add the $5 Todo Mexico Plus with Mexico Roaming add-on to your account and you are currently located in the US, your handset will automatically update with the programming required to enable roaming in Mexico.

If you are already in Mexico and opt to add the $5 Todo Mexico Plus with Mexico Roaming add-on, you will need to connect your device to a Wi-Fi network to access your Boost account through ‘My Account’ to add the service. Once the add-on is added to your account, you will need to update your handset’s profile to receive all required programming for roaming. Follow the steps below for each device type.

Android

  1. Connect to Wi-Fi.
  2. Go to Settings > System Updates > Update Profile.
  3. Upon successful profile update the device may reboot.
  4. Go to Settings > System Updates > PRL Update.
  5. Go to Settings > Mobile Networks > Data Roaming > Roaming Network. Set to Automatic.
  6. Go to Settings > Mobile Networks > Data Roaming > International. Turn on Voice and Data.

iPhone

  1. Connect to Wi-Fi.
  2. Go to Settings > General > About.
  3. Click ‘Carrier’ to check the PRL version.
  4. Go to dialer, enter ##UPDATE# (##873283#), and click dial (green phone icon).
  5. When you see ‘Service Update Complete,’ click ‘OK.’
  6. Go to Settings > Cellular > Roaming.
  7. Enable Voice Roaming.
  8. Enable Data Roaming.

Note that it is strongly advised that you add the $5 Todo Mexico Plus with Mexico Roaming add-on to your account while you are in the US to ensure the updates reach your device properly.

What should I do if I am in Mexico and it seems that my device is not recognizing the roaming network?

It is possible that you will need to power off your handset and then power it back on to reset the device so that it registers on the network.

If you continue to have issues with making calls, SMS or data you will need to check the device settings to ensure International Roaming is enabled.

  • Android devices: Go to Settings > Mobile Networks > Data Roaming > International. Turn on Voice and Data.
  • iPhone devices: Go to Settings > Cellular > Roaming > Enable Voice and/or Data Roaming

If I am in a US/Mexico border city and I have the $5 Todo Mexico Plus with Mexico Roaming add-on, will my device pick up both the Boost and Telefonica networks?

Your phone will try to operate on the network with the best signal and in some areas very close to the border it is possible that the handset will connect to the network in Mexico while you are in the US. The device will indicate on the home screen that it is on the Movistar network if this occurs. It is advised that when you are in the US and this occurs that you modify your settings to ensure you are operating on the preferred network.

Yes, as long as you have purchased the $5 Todo Mexico Plus with Mexico Roaming add-on, you can call local Mexico Emergency Services (066) while traveling in Mexico. You will receive emergency service in Spanish at your Mexico location. Emergency service calls will not incur any access fee or roaming charges.

If you would like more data, you can unsubscribe from the add-on and then re-add it to your account which will restart your data allotment.
