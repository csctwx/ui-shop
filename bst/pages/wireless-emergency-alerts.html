What are Wireless Emergency Alerts/CMAS?

Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA), also known as CMAS, is a part of a national alerting system called IPAWS (Integrated Public Alert and Warning System) that enables emergency management officials to rapidly disseminate the warnings and safety information via text alerts to wireless phones based on the phones’ geographic location, The Federal Emergency Management Agency or FEMA is responsible for receiving the alerting information and forwarding the alerts to participating wireless carriers such as Boost Mobile. Such alerts may come from the President of the United States; the National Weather Service, state or county public safety officials. This system is integrated into the same national alerting services that serve television and radio today. For more information regarding WEA/CMAS please go to: FEMA website.

(top)

Are WEA messages secure and trust worthy?

Yes. All messages are sent from public safety entities that have been certified by their State government and the Department of Homeland Security as having responsibilities relating to the types of messages and geography they are allowed to send.

(top)

What entities will be sending WEAs?

WEAs will be delivered to FEMA by authorized State and local public safety/emergency agencies; by the National Weather Service and other authorized federal public safety/emergency agencies; and by the President of the United States. FEMA will, in turn, deliver such messages to all wireless carriers that have elected to provide WEAs and that have been authorized to connect to the FEMA facility through which the messages are sent.

(top)

What do WEA messages cost?

WEA messages are free. They are a public service brought to you by Boost Mobile in cooperation with Federal, State, and local public safety agencies.

(top)

Are WEA messages the same as SMS text message sent to Boost Mobile phones?

No. Boost Mobile provides wireless services to its customers using the Sprint Nationwide Network. SMS text messages to Boost Mobile CMAS capable phones are sent directly to from a sender to a specific receiving phone, regardless of where on the Sprint Nationwide Network the phone is physically located. WEA messages sent to Boost Mobile phones are sent to a specific geography and broadcast to all WEA capable phones that are on the Sprint Nationwide Network and within the boundaries of the intended message area.

(top)

Are Wireless Emergency Alerts/CMAS available on all Boost Mobile stageices?

No. WEA messages require capabilities that are only available on select CDMA Series Boost Mobile phones. Boost Mobile plans to have all future CDMA Series phones support this capability.

(top)

What Boost Mobile phones are WEA capable?

Sanyo Inuendo, How do I turn on the WEA/CMAS client on my WEA capable stageice?

  1. Go to the phone’s dialer
  2. Dial ##CMAS# or ##2627#
  3. Choose to enable the CMAS client
(top)

What are the different types of WEA alerts?

There are 3 different types of Alerts.

  1. Presidential Alerts – This kind of alert can only be sent by the President of the U.S.
  2. Imminent Threat – This kind of alert is broken down into 2 levels: Extreme and Severe. Examples of this type of alert might be a weather alert or a chemical spill.
  3. AMBER Alerts – America’s Missing: Broadcast Emergency Response. AMBER alerts will now also be sent as WEA.
(top)

Can I opt out of WEA Alerts?

You can opt out of all message types, with the exception of Presidential Alerts. Federal Law requires that all WEA capable phones be able to receive Presidential Alerts.

(top)

How do I opt-out of alerts?

If the WEA/CMAS client is turned on, go to messaging options. Under messaging options, you remove the check marks for the message types you wish to opt out of.

If the WEA/CMAS client is not turned on, no messages will be displayed on the phone.

(top)

How does Boost Mobile know whether to send me an alert?

WEA are delivered based upon geography and are not sent to specific phones. If you are physically located in an area where a WEA message is intended to be delivered and you are within the Sprint Nationwide Network coverage area, your phone will automatically receive and display the message.

(top)

Can I receive WEA on my Boost Mobile phone anywhere on the Sprint Nationwide Wireless Network?

Sprint has enabled WEA nationwide on the Sprint Nationwide Wireless Network, with the exception of Puerto Rico. Sprint plans to enable Puerto Rico in the near future.

(top)

When will my local public safety entities start sending messages?

While Boost Mobile has enabled WEA to be available anywhere on the Sprint Nationwide Wireless Network, Boost Mobile does not control when local and Federal agencies begin sending messages. Please go to the FEMA website or contact your local public safety agency for more information on when they will be participating.

(top)

What if I travel into an impacted emergency area after an alert was sent?

Every WEA has an expiration date/time. Boost Mobile will resend the wireless alert every 5 minutes until the WEA has expired, so even if you travel into an area designated for a WEA after it was originally sent you will still receive the alert. Even though Boost Mobile rebroadcasts the WEA every 5 minutes, your stageice will only display a WEA once.

(top)

How do I tell the difference between a WEA message and a regular text message?

Due to the importance of WEA to public safety, WEA has a distinct vibration cadence and audio tone that are different than you can choose for a regular text message. The audio tone is very similar to what you have heard when emergency alerts are broadcast on television and radio.

WEA also are distinguished in the messaging inbox with a distinctive alert icon which visually defines it as an important WEA.

(top)

Will I receive a WEA message if I am roaming outside of the Sprint Nationwide Wireless Network coverage area?

No. Since Boost Mobile does not have roaming, you will not receive WEA messages if you are outside of the Nationwide Wireless Network coverage area.
